HOUSING DISCRIMINATION

DATA RESEARCH ON THE POVERTY TRAP CYCLE

OVERVIEW

THIS RESEARCH PROJECT INVESTIGATES THE COMPLEX RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN HOUSING DISCRIMINATION AND THE PERPETUATION OF POVERTY. THROUGH RIGOROUS DATA ANALYSIS AND VISUALIZATION, THE PROJECT UNCOVERS PATTERNS AND CORRELATIONS THAT HIGHLIGHT HOW DISCRIMINATORY HOUSING PRACTICES CREATE AND MAINTAIN SYSTEMIC BARRIERS TO ECONOMIC MOBILITY. THE FINDINGS ARE PRESENTED THROUGH AN INTERACTIVE WEBSITE DESIGNED TO RAISE AWARENESS AND INFORM POLICY DISCUSSIONS.

RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

  • DATA COLLECTION — SOURCED PUBLICLY AVAILABLE DATASETS ON HOUSING PATTERNS AND POVERTY INDICATORS
  • STATISTICAL ANALYSIS — PYTHON AND R FOR CORRELATION ANALYSIS AND TREND IDENTIFICATION
  • DATABASE MANAGEMENT — SQL FOR DATA ORGANIZATION AND QUERYING
  • DATA VISUALIZATION — LOOKER STUDIO FOR INTERACTIVE DASHBOARDS AND VISUAL INSIGHTS
  • WEB PRESENTATION — CUSTOM HTML/CSS/JAVASCRIPT WEBSITE FOR FINDINGS DISSEMINATION
  • ACCESSIBILITY FOCUS — DESIGNED WITH CLEAR, IMPACTFUL VISUALIZATIONS FOR GENERAL AUDIENCES

IMPACT & SIGNIFICANCE

THIS PROJECT DEMONSTRATES MY COMMITMENT TO USING TECHNOLOGY AND DATA ANALYSIS FOR SOCIAL GOOD. BY MAKING COMPLEX RESEARCH ACCESSIBLE THROUGH INTERACTIVE VISUALIZATIONS AND A THOUGHTFULLY DESIGNED WEBSITE, THE PROJECT AIMS TO RAISE AWARENESS ABOUT SYSTEMIC INEQUITIES AND INFORM POLICY DISCUSSIONS. THIS WORK REFLECTS MY BELIEF THAT TECHNOLOGY SHOULD BE DEPLOYED TO UNDERSTAND AND ADDRESS REAL-WORLD CHALLENGES AFFECTING COMMUNITIES.
